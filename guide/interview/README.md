```markdown
# Interview Guide

This folder contains a structured set of interview questions designed to help capture a person’s life story.

These questions are intended to make it easier to begin recording family history, especially for people who are unsure what to ask or how to start.

## How to Use This Guide

You do not need to follow this exactly or ask every question.

A simple approach is:

1. Choose a quiet setting
2. Use a phone or recorder
3. Start with the timeline questions
4. Let the conversation flow naturally
5. Use other sections as prompts when needed

The goal is not perfection. The goal is to capture real memories while they are still available.

## Sections

The questions are grouped into several categories:

- **Timeline questions** — life events in order (childhood, work, family, major transitions)
- **Sensitive questions** — difficult or complex topics that may require care
- **Favorites questions** — preferences, habits, and personal details
- **Generational questions** — reflections on changes across time
- **Clearing the air** — unresolved issues or things left unsaid

You can move between sections depending on how the conversation develops.

## Recording Tips

- Record audio whenever possible
- Ask open-ended questions
- Avoid interrupting
- Let pauses happen — people often continue after thinking
- Follow interesting details rather than rigidly sticking to a list

Even a single recorded session can provide valuable material.

## After the Interview

Once recorded:

1. Transcribe the audio (manually or with AI tools)
2. Save transcripts as plain text files
3. Add them to your project as **primary sources**

These transcripts become the foundation for building a biography using the workflow.

## Important Note

You do not need a large amount of material to begin.

Even a few recorded conversations can be enough to start building a structured narrative.
```
