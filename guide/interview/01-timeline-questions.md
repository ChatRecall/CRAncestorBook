# Timeline Questions

> **Interviewer Notes:**
> - It can be helpful to supplement the conversation with documents, photos, letters, or other resources.
> - The most important thing is to capture their individual experiences and personality.

---

## I. Early Life and Childhood

- Where and when were you born?
- Can you tell me about your parents and siblings?
- What are your earliest memories?
- Can you describe the house where you grew up?
- What were your favorite activities or hobbies as a child?
- Can you share any memorable stories from your childhood, both personal and related to world events?
- Can you share a memorable family story from your childhood?
- How was school for you? Any favorite subjects or teachers?

---

## II. Adolescence and Personal Development

- How was your experience as a teenager?
- Can you share any important events, experiences, or shifts in world views from your high school years?
- Did you have a high school sweetheart and did that impact your future relationships?
- Did you have any jobs during your teenage years?
- Can you tell me about your friends during that time?
- Can you share a memorable family story from your teenage years?

---

## III. Early Adulthood and Life Choices

- Did you attend college or have any other form of higher education? If yes:
    - Can you share your experiences?
    - Were you a good student, a partier, an athlete, or some combination of them?
    - What was your major and did it change?
    - How did that experience impact your future life?
- Can you tell me about your first job?
- When and how did you meet your spouse/partner?
- What were some of the significant events, personal or world-related, during this time?
- Can you share a memorable family story from your early adulthood?
- Why did you make certain choices in your life?

---

## IV. Mid-Life and Achievements

- Can you describe your career path? What were some of the highlights?
- How was your experience of parenthood?
- Can you share any significant events, personal or world-related, during this period?
- How have you dealt with life's challenges and setbacks?
- Can you share a memorable family story from your mid-life years?
- What do you consider your biggest achievements?
- What life lessons (advice or wisdom) would you want to pass on for younger generations?

---

## V. Later Life and Legacy

- How has retirement been for you? What do you do to keep yourself busy?
- Can you share about your grandchildren?
- Can you share any memorable stories, both personal and world-related, from recent years?
- Can you share a memorable family story from your later years?
- How have you handled aging and health challenges?
- What are you most proud of?
- What values are most important to you?
- How would you like to be remembered?
