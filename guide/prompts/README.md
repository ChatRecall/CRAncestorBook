# Prompts

This folder contains prompts used by the CRAncestorBook workflow.

The goal of including them is transparency. CRAncestorBook is not based on a single hidden “magic prompt.” It works through a staged process made up of smaller prompts and structured pipeline steps.

## Why These Are Included

The prompts are provided so you can:

- see how the workflow is structured
- understand how different stages of the book are generated and refined
- adapt the workflow for your own family-history project
- inspect the system rather than treating it as a black box

## What the Prompts Do

Depending on the stage, prompts may be used to help with tasks such as:

- generating a table of contents
- drafting chapter text
- improving factual coverage
- reducing duplication across chapters
- refining narrative style
- expanding selected episodes from source material

## Important Note

These prompts are part of a larger workflow. Reading them individually may not fully explain how the system works unless you also look at:

- the main README
- `guide/START_HERE.md`
- the program entry points
- the chapter and TOC pipeline code

## Adapting the Prompts

You are welcome to inspect and adapt the prompts for your own use.

However, prompt quality depends heavily on:

- the structure of the surrounding workflow
- the quality of the source material
- the review and editing that happens after generation
